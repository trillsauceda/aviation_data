How is air travel safer than car travel?
Data taken in the last 15 years has shown that traveling by air is much safer and much more cost-effective than getting to the same destination via car or other modes of transport.

Between 1994 and 2009, the average number of fatalities in total from aircraft crashes is 20.106 people.

This compared to data for passenger cars in the same time frame is significantly less than the average number of fatal car crashes which sits at a steep 26,331 people. This means that you are much more likely to die in a car crash than in an airplane.

Thanks to strict regulation and safety measures, aircraft are designed, manufactured and operated with the safety of the passengers in mind.

Between 2006 and 2016 alone, there have been 6,434 air traffic fatalities worldwide, with 2015 being the safest having 185 recorded fatalities WORLDWIDE.
